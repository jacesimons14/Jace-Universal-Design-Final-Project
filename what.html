What is it?

Universal Design is the design of websites and other software to make them accessible to all people, regardless of age or disability

On the web, this means creating experiences that work for:

  • Screen readers and assistive tech
  • Slow connections and old devices
  • Mobile, tablet, and desktop
  • Humans of all abilities

Universal Design vs. Traditional Design

Feature Traditional Design Universal Design
Target Audience "Average" User All Users
Accessibility Afterthought / Retrofitted Built-in from start
Flexibility One size fits all Adaptable to needs
Cost High retrofit costs Lower long-term costs
